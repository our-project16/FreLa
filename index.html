Frela: Frequency-Layered Audio Watermarking for Robust Content Authentication

Comprehensive Experimental Evaluation

Abstract

In this paper, we present \ours, a frequency-layered audio watermarking method for robust content authentication. FreLa targets the growing misuse of online audio, such as voice cloning and generative model synthesis by embedding imperceptible identifiers directly into speech signals. Unlike existing schemes that concentrate watermark energy within a narrow spectral region, FreLa decomposes speech into multiple contiguous frequency bands and embeds a localized watermark in each band using a learned generatorâ€“detector pair. This frequency-layered design creates redundant watermark evidence across the spectrum, so that even if certain bands are distorted, filtered, or removed by downstream processing, sufficient watermark information remains in the unaffected bands to enable reliable detection and tampering localization. We evaluate FreLa on standard speech dataset under 21 real-world transformations, including additive noise, filtering, pitch and speed changes, amplitude scaling, temporal edits, sampling distortions, neural coder compression, and AI-based post-processing. Experimental results show that FreLa preserves high perceptual quality while consistently achieving near-perfect detection and tampering identification, and it outperforms state-of-the-art audio watermarking methods, especially under frequency- and pitch-related attacks. These results demonstrate that FreLa is an effective strategy for building resilient audio watermarking systems that maintain both robustness and imperceptibility in practical deployments.


Keywords: Audio watermarking; content authentication; voice transformations
